Google search

  • Duckduckgo (recommended): Duckduckgo uses Bing, Yahoo and Yandex results Duckduckgo also doesn't track you Duckduckgo.com
  • Swisscows: Swisscows uses bing results just like duckduckgo and also doesn't track you but if you like accurate results I recommend Duckduckgo or Startpage but if you need absolute privacy swisscows is for you Swisscows.ch

Google Chrome/Safari/Edge/Others

  • Mozilla Firefox (recommended): Mozilla Firefox is fast, private and very secure. You should enable DNS over HTTPS to stop your ISP from spying on youFirefox.com
  • Tor browser: The tor browser is based on Firefox but uses the Tor network for ultimate privacy but very slow speeds torproject.org

Enable DNS over HTTPS in Firefox or Chrome

Recommended addons for Firefox

I recommend some addons to increase tracking protection in Firefox

  • Ublock Origin
  • Privacy badger
  • Decentraleyes: Decentraleyes blocks CDN tracking
  • Duckduckgo privacy essentials: DDG privacy essentials sets your default search engine to duckduckgo as well as upgrading insecure HTTP connections to HTTPS if available. It also has a tracker blocker but I don't recommend using it as it misses a lot of trackers

If you still need to use Chrome

  • Privacy badger by EFF: Privacy badger blocks trackers as well as invisible trackers
  • Disconnect: Disconnect is the tracking prevention system Mozilla uses in Firefox
  • Ublock origin: Ublock origin blocks ads and trackers

Google Public DNS or any other DNS


  • Cloudflare 1.1.1.1 (recommended): Cloudflare 1.1.1.1 DNS resolver is very fast1.1.1.1 website
  • Ad guard: Adguard DNS is privacy friendly. By default it blocks adsAdguard.com
  • NextDNS: NextDNS is focused on privacy, security and customizability, you can choose from a list of pre made filters or make your own on what content you want to allow and what content you don't NextDNS.io

Twitter/Facebook

There aren't many privacy friendly social media platforms out there but there are a few less popular ones

  • Mastodon: Mastodon is similar to twitter joinmastodon.org
  • Friendica: Friendica is similar to Facebookfrendi.ca

Messaging

There are many alternatives to popular messaging services such as WhatsApp and Facebook messenger

  • Signal: Signal is similar to WhatsApp and uses end-to-end encryption. Signal support video calling, messaging and voice calls Signal.org
  • (IOS only) Imessage is an apple only product but uses end-to-end encryption, Imessage only supports messaging

YouTube

  • PeerTube: PeerTube is open source and has no ads joinpeertube.org
  • LBRY: LBRY is community controlled, decentralized and focused on content freedom lbry.com

LBRY has a lot of fake content or fear mongering content please don't believe everything you see on LBRY

Microsoft Office

Microsoft office has been in violation of GDPR multiple times. Germany even banned it from it's school system

  • Libreoffice (recommended): Libreoffice is an open source word processor with spreadsheet and presentation capabilities Libreoffice.org
  • Apple iWork: Apple iWork is built into IOS, Mac OS and iPad OS it includes a word processor, spreadsheet maker and presentation maker
  • OpenOffice: OpenOffice is an open source word processor, spreadsheet maker and presentation maker made by Apache openoffice.org

Gmail/Yahoo

Yahoo mail and Gmail scan your email for ad targeting

  • Protonmail (recommended): Protonmail is the most popular encrypted email service. It's hosted in Switzerland. Protonmail.com
  • Tutanota mail: Tutanota mail is encrypted email just like Protonmail. tutanota.com

Outlook mail client

  • Mozilla Thunderbird (recommended): Thunderbird mail is an open source email client developed by the same people as the Firefox browser thunderbird.net
  • Apple mail: Apple mail is included in Mac os, iPad os and IOS

Google Drive/OneDrive/Dropbox

  • Mega.nz: Mega.nz offers 15GB of free end-to-end encryption cloud storage mega.nz
  • Nextcloud (self hosted): Nextcloud allows you to host your own cloud storage although they do have the option to rent cloud storage off third party vendors nextcloud.com

Chrome password manager/Lastpass

  • Bitwarden (recommended): Bitwarden is a free, open source and secure password manager, It has extensions for Firefox, Safari, Chrome, Edge, Tor, Brave, Opera and Vivaldi as well as programs for Windows, Mac os, Linux, IOS and Android. They offer a self hosted option as well as a free cloud hosted option with end-to-end encryption on Microsoft Azure. Bitwarden.com

Google Analytics

Google Analytics tracks your users across the web even if you opt of all the data sharing

  • Plausible Analytics (recommended): Plausible analytics is an EU hosted privacy friendly alternative to Google Analytics. Plans start at 5 EURO a month for up to 10K page views a month, they allow you to go above the 10k page visits for two months if you go over for more then two months they will contact you to work out a solution before they terminate your account plausible.io
  • Simple analytics: Simple analytics is similar to plausible although plans start at 16 EURO a month for 10k page views simpleanalytics.com
  • Matomo on cloud: Matomo on cloud gives you way more data but is not GDPR compliant by default and may require a consent banner but can be configured to not need a consent banner. Plans start at 16 EURO a month. Matomo can be self hosted although I do not recommend this unless you know what your doing as you data security is at risk if you configure the server wrong. matomo.org
